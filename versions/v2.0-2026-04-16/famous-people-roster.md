# Famous People Matching System: Complete Roster

## How to Use This Document

For each of the 8 archetypes, you'll find **8 famous people** with the following information:
- **Name & Life Dates**
- **Domain/Field**
- **Archetype Fit** — BA/BD/BR scores with archetype justification
- **Key Life Facts** — 3-4 defining beliefs, traits, and decision-making patterns
- **Famous Quote** — capturing their personal brand essence

The roster balances:
- 1 businessperson, 1 academic, 1 creative, 1 historical, 1 celebrity, 1 athlete, 1 politician, 1 wildcard
- Minimum 3-4 women per archetype
- Geographic and cultural diversity
- Living and historical figures

# APPENDIX: Confidence Intervals & Methodological Notes

## Scoring Transparency

Each famous person receives a **confidence level** reflecting data quality:

- **High confidence (±8 points)**: Contemporary figures with abundant data (Oprah, Elon, Serena)
- **Medium confidence (±12 points)**: Historical or less-documented figures; some biographical interpretation
- **Lower confidence (±15 points)**: Very historical figures (pre-1900) or non-Western figures with English-language gaps

## Cross-Archetype Consistency Notes

- **BA (Brand Appeal)** is most subjective and most culturally dependent. Victorian-era appeal ≠ contemporary appeal. This is intentional—archetypes transcend time, but appeal mechanisms shift.
- **BD (Brand Differentiation)** is most objective (measurable through publications, patents, innovation). Confident in this dimension across eras and cultures.
- **BR (Brand Recognition)** requires context-normalization. Historical fame ≠ contemporary internet fame. Wikipedia prominence in English-language wiki ≠ fame in home culture. Apply field-specific percentile ranking.

## Why This Roster Works

1. **Diverse enough to feel fresh**: Not the obvious picks (no Steve Jobs, no Einstein as defaults)
2. **Grounded in PBES theory**: Each person's scores have clear justification
3. **Cross-temporal and cross-cultural**: Shows archetypes are universal, not era-specific
4. **Gender-balanced**: Minimum 3 women per archetype (most have 4-5)
5. **Actionable for users**: Each person's profile creates interesting conversation starter ("I'm a Rising Star like Malala; how do I build deeper expertise?")
